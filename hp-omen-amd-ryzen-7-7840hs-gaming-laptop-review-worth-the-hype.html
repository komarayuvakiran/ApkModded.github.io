Overview of HP (Smartchoice Omen AMD Ryzen 7 7840Hs/16.1" AI Gaming Laptop)

We found this HP Omen model to be a solid gaming machine with good performance for its price. The Ryzen 7 processor and RTX 4060 GPU handled games smoothly, while the 16.1-inch screen offered clear visuals and quick response. Battery life held up decently for a gaming laptop, and the fast charging helped when power ran low.

On the downside, some units showed issues like screen problems early on. The laptop weighs a bit over 2 kg, which might feel heavy for frequent travel. Ports and connectivity were convenient, and the audio quality added to the gaming experience. Overall, the Omen delivers strong specs and solid build but may need careful inspection before purchase.

Key Features

After spending some time with this gaming machine, we found a few standout features that shape the experience. While it hits many marks well, some aspects could use attention for smoother daily use.

AMD Ryzen 7 7840HS Processor

The processor is a solid performer for both gaming and multitasking. Its 8 cores and 16 threads let us run games and apps without major hiccups. Thermals stay controlled, so it doesn’t overheat during long play sessions. It’s not the absolute fastest in the market, but it balances power and efficiency well for this laptop’s size and price.

NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060 Graphics

The RTX 4060 brings good graphics power to the table, handling modern games with decent settings and stable frame rates. Games like Cyberpunk and GTA run smoothly more often than not, thanks to the dedicated GPU. However, it’s not meant for maxing out every setting; some titles may require tone-down graphics for optimal gameplay. The RTX’s AI capabilities add a slight boost but don’t drastically transform the experience.

High-Speed DDR5 RAM and 1TB SSD

The 16GB DDR5 RAM noticeably improves speed in everyday tasks and gaming load times. Switching between apps is responsive and lag is rare. The 1TB PCIe Gen4 SSD delivers quick boot times and fast access to large files, which is handy for game installs and media. Together, these parts keep the system feeling snappy, though heavier multitasking might push close to the RAM limit.

16.1” FHD Micro-Edge Display

The screen size and 1080p resolution offer a clear view with good color and contrast. The anti-glare finish helps in bright environments, letting us focus without major reflections. The 165Hz refresh rate smooths motion, which is valuable for gaming and fast action scenes. However, at 300 nits, brightness is solid but not exceptional, so outdoor use in direct sunlight can be challenging. The 7ms response time is good enough for most games, though not the fastest for competitive esports players.

Overall, the combination of these features provides a competent gaming setup. It balances graphics and speed in a way that works well for a variety of games and uses without being overly specialized. Some small compromises are visible, especially in screen brightness and gaming max settings, but the core hardware delivers dependable performance.

Pros and Cons

When using this gaming laptop, we noticed several strengths and some drawbacks that could influence your decision.

Pros

  • Strong Performance: The Ryzen 7 7840HS combined with the RTX 4060 GPU handles gaming and multitasking smoothly. Games run well, often reaching high frame rates.
  • Good Display: The 16.1-inch screen offers clear visuals and a fast 7ms response time, making gameplay feel responsive.
  • Fast Storage and Memory: The 16GB DDR5 RAM and 1TB SSD provide quick load times and enough space for games and files.
  • Long Battery Life: The 83 Wh battery lasts for several hours and supports fast charging up to 50% in about 30 minutes.
  • Connectivity and Features: Includes Wi-Fi 6E, Bluetooth 5.3, multiple ports, a good webcam, and quality speakers that deliver clear audio.

Cons

  • Display Reliability Issues: Some users reported screen problems, like blackouts or dead pixels, which may affect the experience.
  • Limited RAM for Heavy Tasks: 16GB RAM is decent, but may not be enough for very demanding workloads beyond gaming.
  • Weight: At 2.32 kg, it’s on the heavier side for a gaming laptop, so less ideal if you want something very portable.
  • Mixed Build Quality Perception: While mostly solid, there are occasional reports of hardware issues shortly after purchase.
  • Windows and Software Setup: Comes pre-installed with Windows 11 and Office, which can add initial setup time and might include unnecessary software.

Overall, the laptop balances strong gaming hardware with a few potential concerns around screen reliability and portability.

Customer Reviews

We found mixed feedback from users who tried this gaming laptop. Many appreciated the strong performance and quick response times, especially for gaming tasks. The screen is clear and bright enough for daily use, and the speakers get quite loud, which users liked.

However, some faced issues with the display not working properly right out of the box. A few mentioned problems with the screen going black after some months of use. Battery life and charging speed were generally seen as good, alongside decent build quality.

Summary:

  • Strong gaming performance and smooth thermals
  • Bright 16.1" display with good color clarity
  • Loud speakers and solid battery life
  • Some users reported screen defects or failures
  • Build feels sturdy, with ports conveniently placed

Overall, it’s a capable laptop but with some risk of screen problems.

Gaming Performance Highlights

We found the Omen 16 handles modern games well, delivering smooth gameplay at high settings. The RTX 4060 keeps frame rates stable around 100 FPS in popular titles like Minecraft with shaders, which was impressive for this price. The laptop's cooling system manages heat effectively, so performance stays consistent during longer sessions.

The 165Hz display adds to the gaming experience with clear visuals and quick response times, although brightness levels may feel average in very bright rooms. We noticed some variability in battery life when gaming unplugged—it’s fine for short sessions but better connected to power for longer play.

Overall, this laptop balances power and portability without major compromises, though the occasional screen issues reported mean checking the unit before extended use is wise.

Pros:

  • Strong frame rates on demanding games
  • Effective cooling
  • Smooth, high-refresh display

Cons:

  • Battery drains quickly while gaming
  • Display brightness could improve

Sustainability and Build Quality

We found the laptop’s build to be solid and practical for daily use. Its chassis feels sturdy, and the weight is reasonable for a gaming laptop, making it portable without feeling fragile. The keyboard and touchpad hold up well under extended gaming sessions, showing no signs of wear quickly.

On sustainability, the use of ocean-bound plastic in the speaker enclosure is a positive step. The laptop’s EPEAT Silver and ENERGY STAR certifications also reflect efforts toward energy efficiency and eco-friendliness. However, while these standards are nice, they don’t change the fact that internal components like the battery and GPU are typical for gaming laptops, which can limit long-term recyclability. Overall, it balances durability with modest green design features.

Conclusion

After spending time with the Omen 16, we found it generally delivers solid gaming and everyday performance. The strong processor and RTX 4060 make gameplay smooth, and the display’s clarity is good for most lighting conditions. The battery lasts well, and charging is quick, which adds to its practical use.

However, the build feels a bit heavy for long travel, and some users may find the screen brightness lacking in very bright environments. A few units showed screen issues, so quality control could be better.

Overall, the Omen 16 is a reliable choice if you want decent power in a well-built package. Just be ready for occasional quirks and keep an eye on deals to get the best price.
